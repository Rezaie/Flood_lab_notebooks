Predictability of storm surge and Chesapeake Bay flooding: from days to weeks Notebook

Description:¶

  • Run the iFlood SubX forecast system over a period for which SubX re-forecasts have been produced (1999-2015) to evaluate the overall predictability statistics of sea-level predictions at sites along the Chesapeake Bay where historical tide gauge data is available for validation, and the predictability at various lead times for individual events.
  • Evaluation of the predictability at the daily, weekly, up to 1-month timescale of the key variables, forecasted by the SubX models, that are needed to force the Mason iFlood system - namely 10-meter winds and sea-level pressure over the western North Atlantic. For this task we will look at the overall predictability statistics for the 1999-2015 re-forecast period over which the global weather and climate models participating in the SubX project have been run. We will evaluate the skill of the multi-model mean as well as the respective model ensembles.
  • We used GEFS ensemble 10 to test as a forcing for our system. It is then being compared with the Validated Data provided.
